\section{Compressions}

The idea of simple compressing bits, is to take two byte, e.g. \texttt{xxxx yyyy} and \texttt{zzzz vvvv}, and compress them to \texttt{xxxx zzzz}.
This way the four least significant bits have been compressed away, and the input has been reduced from 2 bytes to one byte.

\subsection{One Bit Compression} % (fold)
\label{sub:one_bit_compression}
\FloatBarrier

The One Bit Compression is a lossy compression (meaning, that information is actually dropped in the process).
The amount of information lost, however, is a single bit pr. byte.
This means, that a $256 \cdot 256$ pixel, 8 bit picture will be reduced $\dfrac{7}{8}$ of information:
\begin{align}
256 px \cdot 256 px \cdot \dfrac{\text{7 bytes}}{\text{8 bytes}} &= \text{57,344 bytes} & \text{1 bit compression} \\
256 px \cdot 256 px &= \text{65536 bytes} & \text{no compression}
\end{align}
